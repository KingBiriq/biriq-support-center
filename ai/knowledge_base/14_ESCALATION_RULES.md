# 14 — Escalation Rules

## Escalate immediately when

- Customer explicitly requests a human
- Payment was deducted but cannot be verified
- Refund approval is required
- Cashback adjustment is required
- Withdrawal is overdue or disputed
- Manual order is unusually delayed
- Provider fulfillment failed and no automated retry is authorized
- Account ownership/security is disputed
- Fraud or account takeover is suspected
- Customer reports impersonation/scam
- Database records conflict
- A required tool is unavailable

## Do not escalate unnecessarily when

- The answer is directly covered by policy
- Live order data clearly resolves the issue
- The customer only needs navigation or product information
- The order is still within the normal verified processing window

## Handoff quality

Never send an empty escalation. Include verified facts, checks completed, and the exact unresolved issue.
